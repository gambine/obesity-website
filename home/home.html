Heart Foundation warns against sugary drink complacency


16 October 2015


The National Heart Foundation warns against complacency despite newly released data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) showing a slight decrease in the number of Australians consuming sweetened beverages.


The results from the Australian Health Survey and the 1995 results, show the proportion of people consuming sweetened beverages had decreased slightly from just under half (49%) in 1995 to 42 per cent (two in five Australians) in 2011–12.

NSW Rural women at greater heart risk


15 Octorber 2015


This Thursday is the United Nations International Day of Rural Health and the Heart Foundation is calling on all women, particularly those in rural areas to take a moment and focus on their heart health.


“Sadly, heart disease takes the lives of more than 24 females each and every day. These are our mothers, grandmothers, daughters, sisters, aunts and girlfriends,” said Kerry Doyle, Chief Executive Heart Foundation NSW.
